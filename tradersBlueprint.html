Model Explained

Shinobi's high win-rate SMC strategy combines two of the most powerful ICT concepts — Inverted Fair Value Gaps and Order Blocks — into one precision trading model used to trade ES and NQ futures consistently.

SMC

What Is Smart Money Concepts?

Smart Money Concepts (SMC) is a trading methodology based on the theory that markets are primarily driven by institutional order flow — banks, hedge funds, and large financial institutions. Rather than following retail indicators, SMC traders read price through the lens of how institutions build, execute, and protect large positions. This includes concepts like market structure, liquidity grabs, imbalances, order blocks, and fair value gaps.

OB

Order Blocks — Institutional Footprints

Order Blocks (OBs) are specific candlestick patterns that represent areas where institutional traders placed large orders, creating significant supply or demand zones. When price returns to these levels, institutions often re-enter their positions — making OBs reliable high-probability entry zones with tight, defined stop losses. Shinobi's model specifically identifies demand OBs in bullish market structure and supply OBs in bearish structure for the cleanest possible entries.

IFVG

Inverted Fair Value Gaps — Precision Entries

A Fair Value Gap (FVG) is a 3-candle price imbalance where the market moved too quickly for full price discovery. When price returns to "fill" this gap and then inverts it — crossing beyond the original gap boundary — the zone becomes an Inverted FVG (IFVG). This inversion signals a high-probability continuation in the original direction, providing surgical entry points with exceptional risk-to-reward. Combined with OB confluence, IFVG entries are among the most precise in all of SMC trading.

📐

The Full IFVG + OB Confluence Model

Shinobi's edge comes from combining both concepts — only entering when an IFVG appears within or near a valid Order Block. This confluence dramatically increases the probability of any single setup, as you're entering at a zone that simultaneously represents institutional demand/supply (OB) and a fresh price imbalance (IFVG). The result is a high win-rate model that can be applied mechanically once learned, enabling consistent profitability even with tight daily drawdown limits.

Why The IFVG+OB Model Works
✓

Institutional confluence — when an Order Block (institutional demand/supply) and an IFVG (price imbalance) overlap, you have two independent reasons to expect a reaction at the same price level — dramatically increasing probability.

✓

Tight risk, high reward — OBs provide clear invalidation levels for stop losses, and IFVGs target subsequent imbalances as profit targets. The natural structure of the setup often delivers 3:1 to 10:1 R:R ratios on confirmed entries.

✓

Repeatability — markets create OBs and IFVGs every single day across every timeframe. Once you can see them, there's never a shortage of setups — just a matter of waiting for the ones with the highest confluence and cleanest structure.

✓

Prop firm compatible — the model's mechanical nature, defined entries, and tight stops are perfectly aligned with the consistency requirements and daily drawdown limits of major prop firms like TopStep, Apex, and MyFundedFutures.
